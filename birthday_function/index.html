NYC Function Challenges

Function notation is a rule that connects an input to an output. First, explore how each NYC function works by changing the input with the slider. Then, take on the challenge to see if you can use the function's rule to find the right answer!

1. Explore: MTA Travel Time

The function T(s) = 3s + 4 calculates the total travel time in minutes for `s` stops.

2. Challenge: Solve the Problem

Your friend says their trip from Times Sq-42 St to South Ferry took exactly 25 minutes. How many stops did they travel? In other words, solve for `s` when T(s) = 25.

1. Explore: Citibike Rental Cost

The function C(m) = 0.30m + 4.79 calculates the total cost for a ride of `m` minutes.

2. Challenge: Solve the Problem

You're biking from your high school near Union Square to Central Park for 35 minutes to meet a friend. What is the value of C(35), your total cost?

1. Explore: Skyscraper Height

The function H(f) = 12f calculates the elevator's height in feet at floor `f`.

2. Challenge: Solve the Problem

You're visiting an office in the Empire State Building. A sign says you are 480 feet above the ground. What floor are you on? Solve for `f` when H(f) = 480.

1. Explore: Deli Sandwich Price

The function P(t) = 1.50t + 8.00 calculates the price of a sandwich with `t` toppings.

2. Challenge: Solve the Problem

You order a classic sandwich with 3 toppings (lettuce, tomato, and cheese). What is the value of P(3), your total cost?
